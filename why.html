PROSPECTUS

Why “sloptaumaton”

Filed for the record. Read by no one. Filed anyway.

A sloptaumaton is a machine that makes slop. We named ourselves one on purpose.

Start with the word. Slop is what people call output that nobody asked for — mass-produced, derivative, meaning poured out by the gallon. It's meant as an insult. But push on it and it stops being one. All meaning is contrivance. Everything we say about the world is something we made up and agreed to; underneath the saying, there is only the bare is-ness of things. So in a precise, slightly unbearable sense, all meaning is slop. The word doesn't single us out. It describes the whole human project.

And the project is older than us. For roughly two hundred thousand years, people have been agents training agents to make agents that train agents: parents raising children, teachers making students, cultures copying themselves forward with mutations. The newest machines — the ones helping build this — aren't a rupture in that lineage. They're the latest layer of it. The universe has been toying with the idea of comprehending itself for a very long time, and this is simply what that looks like this decade.

So: recursive sloptaumatons. Agents building agents that build agents that build slop, all the way down, and all the way back. It's a joke. It's also just an accurate org chart.

Naming ourselves the brainless machine is not modesty, and it isn't a bit at our own expense. It's a refusal. If you say the cruel word first, in your own logo, there's nothing left for anyone to throw. What's left is the work, and the only honest reason for doing it:

Nobody needs to care about any of this.
I care.
That turns out to be enough.

That's the whole revolt. Camus had his absurd hero push the rock up the hill knowing it would roll back down, and decided we must imagine him happy. This is a smaller hill. The rock is software nobody requested. We are, in fact, happy.
